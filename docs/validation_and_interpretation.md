## Graphical Interpretation Guide

### 1. Yield Curve Topology (`yield_curve.png`)
*   **What it shows**: The current relationship between Treasury maturities (3M to 30Y) compared to historical snapshots (1 month and 6 months ago).
*   **How to read it**: 
    *   **Blue Line (Current)**: Represents the "Now". Look for inversions (short rates higher than long rates) or steepening.
    *   **Gray/Light Gray Lines**: Show the "Path". If the current line is significantly higher than historical lines, it indicates a broad upward shift in yields.
*   **Epistemic Value**: Helps orient the user in the "Yield Space" and identify which part of the curve is moving fastest.

### 2. Attention Surface (`relevance_surface.png`)
*   **What it shows**: A horizontal ranking of market variables based on their "Attention Weight."
*   **How to read it**:
    *   **Longer Bars**: Indicate variables that the engine believes "matter most" right now.
    *   **lambda/beta**: High relevance here suggests that thermodynamic decay or systemic injection is driving the market more than raw price action.
    *   **Z_Score**: High relevance indicates the market is focused on mean-reversion and "stretched" conditions.
*   **Epistemic Value**: Answers the core question: **"What matters right now?"** It helps the user ignore noise and focus on dominant drivers.

### 3. Entropy Dashboard (`entropy_dashboard.png`)
*   **What it shows**: Two subplots tracking market stability over time.
    *   **Top Plot (Shannon Entropy)**: Measures market "disorder." High peaks indicate periods of high uncertainty or rapid regime shifts.
    *   **Bottom Plot (Thermodynamics)**: Tracks `lambda` (the rate at which mispricings decay) and `beta` (the rate at which new entropy is injected into the system).
*   **How to read it**:
    *   When **Entropy** is above its mean, the environment is unstable; defensive postures are preferred.
    *   When **lambda > beta**, the system is "healing" or returning to equilibrium.
    *   When **beta > lambda**, systemic noise is increasing, and predictability is dropping.
*   **Epistemic Value**: Provides a "health check" of the market's informational stability.
